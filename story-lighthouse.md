# Core Narrative (Logline)
A lone survivor awakens in a deserted cliffside village as a sentient storm rises from the sea. To live through the night, they must restore the lighthouse—now a broken seal holding back an otherworldly rift—or improvise a desperate escape before the island is swallowed.

Validation: Centers a concrete objective (restore lighthouse) that drives exploration, resource gathering, and defense, while preserving alternate playstyles.

# Antagonist
## Primary: The Fogmind (aka The Drowned Matriarch) — an ancient mind riding the storm, using the lighthouse lens as a beacon to breach in creatures. It whispers through radios and wind, corrupts electrics, and targets player progress beats.

## Secondary/Systemic: Keeper-9 (lighthouse automation) bound to obsolete protocols; will oppose unsafe activations unless prerequisites are met (forcing quests).

## Presence Loop:
Environmental: siren-like hums, fog pulses that disable lights, wave surges.
Tactical: “Hunt” phases during repairs; targeted spawns at gates/repair sites.
Narrative: taunts on radio; distorted village PA; phantom footprints echoing villagers.

## Validation: Gives the opposition a face, clear agency, and escalating counterplay tied to gameplay triggers.

# Player Origin Options (choose default)
- Investigator (Default): Responded to an automated distress signal; boat wrecked in storm. Carries a field radio and a half-burned map. Motivation: finish the rescue.
- Keeper’s Apprentice: Was stationed here; returns from supply run to find the village empty. Motivation: duty and guilt.
- Castaway: Dragged ashore by the lighthouse glow. Motivation: survive at any cost; origin slowly ties to Fogmind visions.

## Validation: Each origin justifies starting alone; provides tutorial gear; fits amnesia-light setup without clichés.

# Overarching Goals and Win Conditions
## Primary Goal: Restore the Lighthouse Seal (align prism shards, power generator, calibrate beam) before the superstorm peaks.

## Alternate Goals:
- Escape: Repair a boat and broadcast a rescue vector; sustain the beam long enough to guide a ship.
- Endure: Fortify the village and survive three storm cycles until dawn stabilizes the rift temporarily.
- Failure States: Generator overloads, prism misalignment during surge, village core breached (tower/command post falls), or rescue ship lost due to unlit beacon.

## Validation: Multi-path outcomes support different player styles and replayability.

# Structural Outline (Acts and Gated Objectives)
## Act I: Signals in the Fog
Objectives: Find the command shack; restore basic power; access radio; survive first wave.
Gating: Keycard to guard tower; fuse for generator; map fragment.
Lore: Keeper’s logs imply the lens once “held the sea quiet.”

## Act II: The Broken Lens
Objectives: Gather 3 prism shards from chapel crypt, flooded cave, and watch office; repair gates; power towers.
Mini-bosses: Gatebreaker (brute that targets fencing), Siren Twins (lure from chapel bell).
Choice Lock: At two shards, choose path—Contain (seal), Escape (boat), or Endure (fortify).

## Act III: Eye of the Storm
Contain Path: Ascend lighthouse during surge; align lens; fight Fogmind projection circling the prism.
Escape Path: Hold harbor against creature waves; keep beam lit while rescue ship navigates reefs.
Endure Path: Multi-front defense; rotate tower power; temporary beam bursts to repel fog; dawn event.
Epilogue: Variable outcomes reflected in island state, final radio message, and ocean calmness.
Validation: Clear milestones create purposeful exploration; each objective ties to a system (power, defense, traversal).

# Key Locations and Rewards
Lighthouse: Final arena; lens alignment minigame; Fogmind projections.
Generator Station: Risk-reward power routing; overload if mismanaged.
Chapel: Bell puzzles; Siren Twins; prism shard sanctified.
Flooded Cave: Tidal timing; bioluminescent guidance; shard embedded in coral.
Watch Office: Map room; Keeper-9 interface; master gate crank.
Harbor/Boathouse: Hull repair, fuel siphoning, flare gun upgrade.

# Questlines (Actionable)
Light the Way: Restore generator, route power to lighthouse, retrieve control rod, align lens.
Sever the Chorus: Silence chapel bell, defeat Siren Twins, sanctify shard to resist fog pulses.
Hold the Line: Rebuild two gates, calibrate two towers, craft flares to deter night surge.
Open Water: Patch hull, refuel engine, broadcast bearing, protect harbor during approach.
The Old Protocol: Convince/override Keeper-9 via three authorization tokens (tower, watch office, chapel crypt).

# Systems Integration
Power Management: Switchboards that route power between towers, lighthouse, and boathouse; storm surges force re-routing under time pressure.
Defense Economy: Scrap → walls/gates → tower ammo; higher walls slow Gatebreaker but attract larger waves.
Fog Pulses: Periodic visibility/aim debuffs; reduced if lens partially aligned.
Audio Cues: Wind carries antagonist lines; stronger when player is on main objective.

# Antagonist Escalation and Bosses
## Mini-boss Phases:
- Gatebreaker: Punishes over-investment in one gate; teaches distributed defense.
- Siren Twins: Punishes sound reliance; counters by silencing bell/earplugs item.

## Final Boss Variants:
- Fogmind Lensfight (Contain): Circle hazards, phase-shift adds, alignment holds open vulnerability windows.
- Harbor Siege (Escape): Timed waves; beam uptime meter; protect ship hull integrity.
- Town Core Last-Stand (Endure): Three fronts; rotating power; temporary beam bursts as ult.

# Thematic Throughlines
Light vs. Lure: Beacon as salvation or bait.
Duty vs. Survival: Save the island vs. save yourself.
Memory in Weather: Recordings and echoes in wind/fog.

Refusal: Boat beyond repair—for now; storm worsening.
Mentor: Keeper-9 protocols; Keeper’s voice logs.

Reward: Beam re-lit (partial), new radio channel opens.
Road Back: Final prep and defenses; countdown storm timer.
Resurrection: Final battle (path-specific).
Return: Rescue/Seal/Dawn; island transformed.

Antagonist: Fogmind with systemic support from Keeper-9; constant presence loop.
Character Origin: Three options; default Investigator for clarity.
